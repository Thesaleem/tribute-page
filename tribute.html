Timeline of Chadwick Boseman's Life

  • 1976: Chadwick Aaron Boseman was born on November 29, 1976 in Anderson, South Carolina, to Carolyn and Leroy Boseman.
  • 1995: Boseman graduated from High School.
  • 2000: Attended college at Howard University with a Bachelor of Fine Arts in directing.
  • 2002: Graduated from the New York City Digital Film Academy.
  • 2003: Boseman's first television role was in an episode of the NBC crime drama Third Watch.
  • 2008: Appeared in his first feature film, The Express: The Ernie Davis Story.
  • 2013: He appeared in his first starring role in the film 42, where he portrayed Jackie Robinson.
  • 2014: He portrayed musician James Brown in the film Get On Up.
  • 2016: Boseman starred as the Egyptian deity Thoth in the movie Gods of Egypt, and began the role of TChalla in the Marvel franchise film Captain America: Civil War. He was also diagnosed with stage III colon cancer, which later progressed to stage IV.
  • 2018: Boseman became the first African American superhero in a major feature film when he starred in the movie Black Panther, the 12th-highest grossing movie of all time.
  • 2020: Boseman died at his home in Los Angeles after his four-year battle with colon cancer, with his wife and family by his side. He was forty-three years old.
